Summary:
"My First Cousin Once Removed: Money and the Family of Robert Lowell" is a book that delves into the complicated relationship between the famous poet Robert Lowell and his wealthy family. Written by Madness, a close relative of Lowell, the book explores how money and privilege shaped Lowell's life and work, and how his family's dynamics influenced his personal struggles. Through personal anecdotes and insights, the author paints a complex portrait of a family that was both a source of support and conflict for Lowell.
