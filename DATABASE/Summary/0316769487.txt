Title: The Catcher in the Rye
Author: J.D. Salinger
Category: Fiction

Summary:
The Catcher in the Rye is a classic coming-of-age novel by J.D. Salinger that follows the rebellious and troubled teenager, Holden Caulfield, as he navigates through life and struggles with the phoniness of the adult world. Told in first-person narrative, the novel explores themes of alienation, identity, and the loss of innocence, as Holden searches for meaning and connection in a world that he sees as superficial and inauthentic. Published in 1951, The Catcher in the Rye has become a beloved and controversial novel, resonating with readers of all ages.
